History of Economic Thought

History of Economic Thought refers to the origins and evolution of economic ideas and theories from ancient times to present day. It examines the contributions of major economists and their impact on economic policies and practices.

This subject covers the following key areas:

  1. Ancient and Medieval Economic Thought

    2. This point explores economic ideas in ancient civilizations such as Greece, Rome, and China, and during the Middle Ages in Europe.

  2. Mercantilism and the Emergence of Capitalism

    3. This includes the economic policies of European nations from the 16th to 18th centuries and the rise of capitalist systems.

  3. Classical Economic Thought

    4. It focuses on the ideas of Adam Smith, David Ricardo, Thomas Malthus, and John Stuart Mill, who laid the foundations of modern capitalism.

  4. Marxian Economic Thought

    5. This part discusses Karl Marx's critiques of capitalism and his theories on communism and socialism.

  5. Neoclassical and Keynesian Economic Thought

    6. This covers the development of microeconomics and macroeconomics, including the contributions of Alfred Marshall, Leon Walras, and John Maynard Keynes.

  6. Contemporary Economic Thought

    7. This point examines current economic theories, models, and debates, including globalization, inequality, and sustainable development.
