Advanced Microeconomics

Advanced Microeconomics deals with the in-depth analysis of consumer and producer behavior, market structures, and decision-making processes using advanced mathematical and theoretical models.

This subject covers the following key areas:

  1. Consumer Theory

    2. Examines consumer preferences, utility functions, demand curves, and how consumers make optimal choices under budget constraints.

  2. Production and Cost

    3. Focuses on production functions, cost minimization, and the relationship between inputs and outputs in the short and long run.

  3. Market Structures

    4. Studies different types of markets such as perfect competition, monopoly, monopolistic competition, and oligopoly.

  4. Game Theory

    5. Introduces strategic behavior and decision-making among rational players in competitive situations.

  5. General Equilibrium and Welfare Economics

    6. Analyzes the interdependence of markets and how resources are allocated efficiently in an economy.

  6. Uncertainty and Information

    7. Discusses decision-making under uncertainty and the role of information asymmetry in markets.
