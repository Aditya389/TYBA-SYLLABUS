Research Methodology

Research Methodology refers to the structured process of conducting research, including the tools and techniques used to collect, analyze, and interpret data. It enables students to understand the principles of scientific investigation and apply them in economic and social research.

This subject covers the following key areas:

  1. Introduction to Research

    2. This module introduces the meaning, objectives, types, and significance of research, particularly in social sciences and economics.

  2. Research Design and Process

    3. Focuses on identifying a research problem, formulating hypotheses, reviewing literature, and selecting appropriate design and methodology.

  3. Sampling Techniques

    4. Explains types of sampling: probability and non-probability, their advantages and limitations, and how to determine sample size.

  4. Data Collection Methods

    5. Describes primary and secondary data sources, tools such as questionnaires, interviews, and observation, and their applications.

  5. Data Analysis and Interpretation

    6. Involves editing, coding, tabulation, and use of statistical tools for interpreting data meaningfully to derive conclusions.

  6. Report Writing and Presentation

    7. Covers the format, structure, and ethics of writing a research report, referencing styles, and how to present findings effectively.
