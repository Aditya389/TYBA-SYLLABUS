Economics of Growth and Development

Economics of Growth and Development is a branch of economics that focuses on how countries improve their economy and the quality of life of their people. It deals with the reasons for economic progress, the challenges of poverty, and how to promote sustainable development.

This subject covers the following key areas:

  • Meaning and Measurement of Economic Growth

    • This includes how economic growth is defined and measured through indicators like GDP and GNP.

  • Determinants of Economic Growth

    • It discusses factors such as capital formation, human resources, technology, and infrastructure that help an economy grow.

  • Theories of Economic Growth

    • This part explains different growth models like Classical, Harrod-Domar, and Solow models used to understand economic development.

  • Barriers to Economic Development

    • This includes challenges like poverty, inequality, lack of education, and political instability that block development.

  • Role of State and Market

    • It discusses how both government and private sector contribute to economic development.

  • Sustainable Development

    • This section explains how to achieve growth without harming the environment and keeping future generations in mind.

  • Globalization and Economic Development

    • This part covers the impact of global trade and international relations on the economic growth of a country.
