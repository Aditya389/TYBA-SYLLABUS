Environmental Economics

Environmental Economics studies how economic activities affect the environment and how economic tools can be used to solve environmental problems. It analyzes the balance between development and sustainability.

    4. Methods to assign economic value to non-market goods like clean air, water, and biodiversity.

  4. Environmental Policy Instruments

    5. Different tools like taxes, subsidies, permits, and regulations used by governments to control pollution.

  5. Sustainable Development

    6. Concept, indicators, and challenges of achieving development without depleting natural resources.

  6. Global Environmental Issues

    7. Focus on climate change, deforestation, biodiversity loss, and international agreements like the Paris Agreement.
