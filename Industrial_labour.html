Industrial labour

Industrial Labour is a branch of economics that focuses on the working class involved in industries and the economic, social, and legal aspects affecting them. It studies the conditions, rights, problems, and welfare of workers who are engaged in manufacturing and industrial sectors.

This subject covers the following key areas:

  1. Nature and Characteristics of Industrial Labour

    2. This point explains who industrial labourers are and what makes their work unique in the industrial sector.

  2. Labour Problems in India

    3. This includes the common problems faced by industrial workers such as unemployment, poor working conditions, and exploitation.

  3. Wages and Wage Policy

    4. It focuses on how wages are decided in India and the importance of fair and minimum wages for industrial workers.

  4. Trade Unions

    5. This part discusses the role, functions, and importance of trade unions in protecting the rights of workers.

  5. Industrial Disputes and Their Settlement

    6. This includes the causes of disputes between workers and employers and the methods used to solve them.

  6. Labour Legislation in India

    7. This covers important laws made by the government to protect the rights and safety of industrial workers.

  7. Labour Welfare and Social Security

    8. This point explains the welfare schemes and security benefits provided to workers like insurance and pensions.

  8. Role of Government and Labour Policy

    9. It describes how the government plans and implements policies to improve the lives of industrial workers in India.
